# Enclosure Design Best Practices

## Lid Sealing — Tongue-and-Groove
- Tongue width: 1.0–1.5 mm
- Tongue height: 2.0–3.0 mm
- Groove width: tongue + 0.2 mm (0.1 mm clearance/side)
- Groove depth: tongue height + 0.3 mm
- Remaining wall behind groove: minimum 1.0 mm

## Ribs and Gussets (Stiffening)
- Rib thickness: 60–80% of adjacent wall (e.g., 1.5 mm for 2.0 mm wall)
- Rib height: up to 3× wall thickness
- Rib spacing: every 2–3× wall thickness
- Fillet at rib-wall junction: 0.5 mm minimum
- Add ribs to ANY flat panel > 40 mm span

## Ventilation
- Slot width: 1.5–2.0 mm
- Bar width between slots: 2.0 mm minimum
- Hole pattern: 2.0–3.0 mm diameter, spacing = 2× hole diameter
- Needed when components dissipate > 0.5W
- Place inlet vents low, outlet vents high (natural convection)
- Minimum 10% of wall area for vent openings on heat-generating sides

## Cable Entry and Strain Relief
- Strain relief slot: 3.0 mm wide, cable OD + 0.5 mm tall, 2.0 mm bridge
- Grommet hole: cable OD + 1.0 mm

## Cable Routing Channels
- Width: cable OD + 1.0 mm (bundle diameter + 2.0 mm)
- Depth: cable OD (cable sits half-in)
- Bend radius: 5× cable OD (static), 10× cable OD (flexing)
- Clip spacing: every 20–30 mm

## Alignment and Assembly
- Alignment pins: 3.0 mm Ø minimum, length = 2× diameter
- Pin clearance: 0.2 mm (0.1 mm/side)
- Chamfer both pin tip and hole entry: 0.5 mm × 45°
- Use 2 pins minimum for rotational constraint
- Offset one pin from symmetric position by ≥ 3 mm to prevent wrong orientation

## Keying (Anti-Misassembly)
- Asymmetric pin placement OR D-shaped key (flat = 70% of boss Ø)
- Key rib: 1.0 mm wide × 1.0 mm tall
- Always key connectors and orientation-sensitive assemblies

## Labeling
- Debossed text preferred (survives handling, can paint-fill)
- Minimum stroke: 0.5 mm (0.8 mm recommended)
- Depth: 0.6–1.0 mm
- Character height: 4.0 mm minimum (5.0 mm+ for readability)
- Font: sans-serif, bold weight
